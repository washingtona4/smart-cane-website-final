A cane that sees what you can't.

A $50 smart cane that detects ground-level hazards: cords, cables, carpet edges, before you step on them. Real-time, on-device, no cloud required.

The problem

1 in 3 adults over 65 falls every year. More than half of those injuries happen at home, caused not by dramatic accidents, but by a cord across a doorway, a curled carpet edge, a bag someone left on the floor. Existing assistive tech detects falls after they happen. We prevent them before contact.

The device
Compute
Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W
Compact, lightweight, built-in Wi-Fi. Coordinates sensor input and serves alerts locally. $15.
Vision
Sony IMX500 AI Camera
On-sensor neural inference via built-in NPU. YOLO11n runs entirely on the chip — no data leaves the device.
Motion sensing
LSM6DS3TR-C IMU
3-axis accelerometer + gyroscope. Gates inference to stable, grounded cane positions only.
How it works
01

IMU gates the system

The accelerometer watches the Z-axis. When the cane tip contacts the ground and stabilizes, the system arms. When the cane lifts or swings, it stands down, no wasted inference on blurry, unreliable frames.

02

Camera frames the path ahead

The Sony IMX500 sits ~2 ft above ground, angled forward. It captures the 3–5 ft zone where ground-level hazards are most likely to appear before your next step.

03

YOLO11n detects on-sensor in ~245ms

No cloud. No network hop. The model runs on the IMX500's neural processor, returning bounding boxes and confidence scores. Up to 0.91 confidence on extension cords under bright light.

04

Audio alert before contact

A local web server triggers an audio cue the moment a hazard clears the confidence threshold — giving 2–3 steps of advance warning. Supports push notifications to a caregiver's device.

“The hazards are rarely dramatic. A cord left across a doorway, a carpet edge that has curled up over time, a bag someone forgot on the floor. These are the things that actually cause falls indoors, and assistive technology has largely overlooked them.”
